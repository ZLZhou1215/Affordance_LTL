Abstract

Enabling robots to understand and execute ambiguous human instructions in dynamic environments remains a fundamental challenge in robotics. Current approaches fail to maintain semantic consistency when translating high-level intent into robust physical actions—they either rely on rigid pre-defined symbolic representations that cannot adapt to environmental changes, or employ end-to-end learning methods that lack formal guarantees and semantic coherence.

To address this limitation, we propose a hierarchical reactive planning framework that systematically integrates formal methods with dynamic perception. At its core are two synergistic components: VLPGen, which dynamically synthesizes minimal, task-relevant Linear Temporal Logic over Finite Traces (LTL$_f$) specifications from visio-linguistic context—eliminating the need for exhaustive pre-defined propositions; and ContextualAfford, which enables zero-shot functional equivalence reasoning by identifying context-dependent object affordances.

Their integration through a hierarchical reactive architecture ensures both global task coherence and local adaptability, providing distinct recovery mechanisms for different failure modes. We rigorously evaluate our framework through 30 long-horizon real-world robotic tasks and introduce ContextaFF, a comprehensive benchmark dataset of 550 annotated images with pixel-level affordance maps for language-grounded affordance evaluation.

Our system achieves an 87.3% success rate on tasks with dynamic uncertainties, successfully performing complex functional equivalences by reasoning about physical properties—such as leveraging the hard edge of a ruler to cut soft dough when a knife was unavailable—representing a 63 percentage point improvement over non-hierarchical baselines, with 91.2% successful failure recovery. This work provides a principled and scalable solution for deploying robots in semi-structured environments where adaptability and semantic understanding are crucial.

Framework Architecture

Framework Overview: An overview of our hierarchical reactive planning framework. The VLPGen module grounds user intent into a formal LTL$_f$ specification, which guides the Hierarchical Reactive Planner. The planner leverages real-time perceptual information from the ContextualAfford module to ensure robust and adaptive execution.
